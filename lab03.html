Comparison Between Part A and Part B

When comparing Part A and Part B, both approaches offer their own advantages. In Part A, we implemented CSS from scratch, which provided full flexibility in controlling the layout, design, and responsiveness. This allowed for custom solutions tailored to specific needs. However, in Part B, using Bootstrap, the process was more streamlined. Though I had to familiarize myself with Bootstrap's documentation and choose the appropriate classes, it saved time as much of the design and functionality was already built-in, eliminating the need to create everything from scratch.

The challenges with Part A were mostly due to the time and effort required to build everything from scratch. Writing custom CSS and handling responsiveness took significantly longer. In contrast, Part B with Bootstrap presented the initial challenge of learning which classes to use, especially for making the layout responsive. However, once I understood the framework, it was much quicker and more efficient to implement.

While Part B was easier and faster, I appreciate the flexibility of Part A. Even though it was time-consuming, custom CSS allowed for complete creative control, enabling me to design exactly as I wanted without any limitations from pre-defined styles.
